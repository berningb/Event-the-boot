Speaker

"You never lose a dream. It just incubates as a hobby."

Larry Page
CEO of Alphabet

Lawrence "Larry" Page (born March 26, 1973) is an American computer scientist and internet entrepreneur who co-founded Google Inc. with Sergey Brin, and is the CEO of Google's parent company, Alphabet Inc. He announced his intention to step aside a second time in July 2015 to become CEO of Alphabet, under which Google's assets would be reorganized. Under Page, Alphabet is seeking to deliver major advancements in a variety of industries. Page is the inventor of PageRank, Google's best-known search ranking algorithm.

Google has been a very important company in terms of advancing technology, and we're estatic to have someone like Larry to come talk to our graduates. We have had a couple Neumont students go and work for Google including some of our current graduates. It will be very exciting to see what these graduates will create and innovate in the future. Larry will be talking about the importance of having dreams and being able to work on them.
